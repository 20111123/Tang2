COMPOSITION OF TANG

The exact composition of Tang may vary depending on the specific flavor and the region where it is sold, as well as over time due to product reformulations. However, generally, Tang is composed of basic ingredients, including:

Sugar

Tang contains sugar to provide a sweet taste to the drink. The amount of sugar may vary depending on the product version, with some versions containing reduced sugar or sugar substitutes.

Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)

Historically, Tang was marketed as a source of vitamin C. Ascorbic acid is a form of vitamin C that can be added to provide nutritional benefits.

ARTIFICIAL FLAVORS

Tang is known for its citrusy and fruity flavors, such as orange, lemon, strawberry, grape, among others. These flavors are obtained through artificial flavorings.

COLORANTS

Colorants are often added to Tang to give the beverages the characteristic appearance of the corresponding fruit. This may include both natural and artificial colorants.

ACIDULANTS

Acidulants, such as citric acid, malic acid, or tartaric acid, are added to give the drink a sour flavor and enhance the tastes.

ACIDITY REGULATORS

These ingredients are used to control and maintain the pH of the beverage within a specific range.

ANTI-CAKING AGENTS

To prevent the powder from clumping, anti-caking agents like tricalcium phosphate or silica are added.

OTHER INGREDIENTS

Depending on the specific product formulation, other ingredients like minerals, sweeteners, dietary fibers, or other additives may be added.
